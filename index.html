Through The Ages

The following web app goes back in time and highlights some of the most inconic Broadway Musicals from the early 20th century until today. According to the Theater Seat Store, Broadway in New York City has gained so much popularity that attendance to these musicals surpassed ticket sales of the professional sports teams in New York and New Jersey by almost three million admissions. Click through the page and you'll get to listen to some of the best music from very memorable shows, as well as test your knowledge with some Broadway trivia.

Broadway and the Theatre Industry through the Years

1834

The first of the currently existing 41 Broadway Theatres, Lyceum Theatre, opens.

1857

Broadway's first long-run show, The Elves, opened this year.

1860

A new record was set for the longest-running Broadway show. The Seven Sisters showcased over 250 shows during its run on Broadway.

1891

A Trip To Chinatown set a new record for 657 performances on Broadway. In this same year, the Palace Theatre opens.

1903

Hudson Theatre opens.

1910

Lunt-Fontanne Theatre opens.

1911

The Winter Garden Theatre opens.

1912

Both the Cort Theatre and Helen Hayes Theatre open.

1913

Three of the 41 existing theatre opened this year: Booth Theatre, Longacre Theatre, and Sam S. Shubert Theatre.

1917

Broadhurst Theatre opens on West 44th Street.

1918

American Airlines Theatre, the Stephen Soundheim Theatre, and Gerald Schoenfeld Theatre open.

1921

Ambassador Theatre and the Music Box Theatre open.

1922

Abie's Irish Rose opens and brings up themes of Jewish ethnicity and Yiddish themes of the time.

1923

Imperial Theatre opens.

1924

Al Hirschfeld Theatre and Broadway Theatre open.

1925

Four theatres opened up this year: August Wilson Theatre, Samuel J. Friedman Theatre, Eugene O'Neill Theatre, as well as Richard Rogers Theatre. Richard Rogers Theatre is where Hamilton is currently showing.

1926

Belasco Theatre and Brooks Atkinson Theatre open.

1927

A total of five theatres opened this year: John Golden Theatre, Bernard B. Jacobs Theatre, Neil Simon Theatre, and St. James Theatre.

1928

Ethel Barrymore Theatre opens.

1929

Majestic Theatre and Walter Kerr Theare open.

1943

After the Great Depression hit, Broadway was slow and not as popular. The first show that revived the Theatre industry after the depression opened this year: Oklahoma!

1957

One of the most iconic Broadway shows, based off of the storyline from Romeo and Juliet, West Side Story opens. It was nominated for numerous Tony Awards this same year. There have been multiple revivals of this show, and there is another one coming soon.

1964

Fiddler on the Roof opens for an 8-year run.

1965

Vivian Beaumont Theatre opens.

1972

Grease opens for an 8-year run, and Pippin opens for close to 5 years.

1973

Minskoff Theatre opens this year.

1986

Marquis Theatre opens this year.

1988

The longest ever running Broadway show opened this year: Phantom of the Opera. It has also won seven Tony awards.

1991

Miss Saigon opens for a 10-year run. It tells the tragic story of an Asian woman who is abandoned by her American lover.

1994

A tale as old as time is brought to the Broadway stage for a 13-year run with Beauty and the Beast.

1996

Rent opens up on Broadway and is considered to be one of the most, if not the most, successful rock 'n' roll musicals of the time. According to Slate, Rent focused on a specific cultural era. Because of that, the popularity of the show soon died down as other show's popularity saw an increase.

1997

The Lion King opens up this year and has been on Broadway for over 10 years. This same year, New Amsterdam Theatre opens.

1998

Lyric Theatre opens this year.

2001

ABBA takes the stage with Mamma Mia! and its introduction the Broadway stage.

2002

Good morning, Baltimore! Or... New York City. Hairspray opens up for a 7-year run.

2003

Glinda teaches us about popularity, and Elphaba teaches us how to defy gravity when Wicked opens up this year. Our favorite storyline from the Wizard of Oz meets an exciting new twist in this adaptation.

2005

Jersey Boys takes the stage this year and follows the lives of four young musicians through their various triumphs and challenges of the time.

2011

Hasa Diga Eebowai! The Book of Mormon introduces itself to Broadway with a raunchy show following the life of a young Mormon on his mission trip to Africa. According to Slate, The Book of Mormon's use of satirical jokes throughout the show leave viewers wondering how a musical with this raunchy humor is even allowed on Broadway.

2013

Kinky Boots and Come From Away both open this year and are currently still playing at their respective theatres.

2015

"You Will be Found" with Dear Evan Hansen. This Broadway show acts as a coming of age story that focuses on a suicide in the age of social media. Evan Hansen's journey is filled with many challenges, growing up, but a lot of love. However Slate says that the popular cuilture appeal to Dear Evan Hansen might be similar to that of Rent, as it focuses on teenage mental health. This might leave people wondering if this musical is focused on a serious topic or just glamorizing mental illness.

2015

The most talked about show of its time (and most expensive), Hamilton takes us back in history on Alexander Hamilton's journey. With a trendy rap soundtrack, there is reason why New York City is the greatest city in the world.

2015

Waitress shows us what a little bit of baking can do. Opening in 2015, this Broadway show has been captivating audiences with its rags to riches story of Jenna Hunterson and her life full of hardships, obstacles, and love.

2017

Everyone's favorite Disney movie, Frozen was transformed into a Broadway show kicking off in 2017.

2017

Wednesday's are back and better than ever with Regina Geoge. Mean Girls opens up in 2017 and brings back fond, but sometimes traumatizing, memories from one of the most iconic movies of its time.

2019

2019 has been a packed year so far with the introduction of Hadestown to the Theatre District, as well as Moulin Rouge!, and we can't forget about Tootsie. To top off an amazing year, West Side Story will be coming back for yet another revival mid-December.
